UMass Chan Medical School August 2022-February 2023

This involved staying up-to-date with the latest advancements, researching new tools and software, and assessing their potential impact on laboratory inventory management. Additionally, I developed my programming skills by creating scripts to manipulate and validate data, streamlining processes and improving accuracy. Research lab assistant

University of Massachusetts, Lowell October 2018 - August 2021

During my time as a Research Lab Assistant at the University of Massachusetts, Lowell, I had the opportunity to gain valuable experience and develop essential skills. One of my primary responsibilities was processing quantitative data, which involved utilizing both Bash and Python scripts to analyze and interpret large datasets. Additionally, I worked extensively with Big Data Clusters, employing UNIX commands to manipulate and extract relevant information from complex datasets. Another crucial aspect of my role was filtering data and extracting targeted DNA sequences, which required precision and attention to detail. In addition to my technical tasks, I also took pride in supporting my colleagues by offering innovative solutions to problems that arose during our research projects. Furthermore, I had the opportunity to showcase my work by presenting our findings to groups of approximately 20 individuals, honing my communication and presentation skills. Overall, my experience as a Research Lab Assistant at the University of Massachusetts, Lowell provided me with a strong foundation in data processing, problem-solving, and effective communication within a research environment.
